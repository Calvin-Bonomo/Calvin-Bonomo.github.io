About Me

I started programming the Summer after my freshman year of highschool. After watching a video about Minecraft’s world generation, I decided it was time to make my own procedurally generated masterpiece. I ended up writing a two-thousand line buggy and broken mess, and while my hopes of making the next indie game sensation had been dashed, I was hooked. I started making games with Java’s AWT library, learning as much as I could as fast as I could. However, I soon became discontent with AWT’s lack of customization, so in my Junior year, I wrote my own 2D renderer in Java. It wasn’t particularly advanced, but it was enough to make a quick falling sand type game. This is the project that got me hooked on graphics programming. I started writing pixel art shaders in Unity before diving head first into C++ and OpenGL. Since then, I published a game for a game jam and have continued my work with rendering APIs.
